Constitution of Pylyp Orlyk

The Bendery Constitution, also known as the Constitution of Pylyp Orlyk, is one of the oldest constitutions in the world and the first constitution of Ukraine. This Constitution was written in old Ukrainian and Latin by Pylyp Orlyk, an elected Hetman. This constitution remains an important symbol for Ukraine and its existence. The constitution referred to the Cossack state as Ukraine. This document is considered a monument of Ukrainian political-philosophical and legal thought, the first modern constitution and the basis of many world constitutions, historians consider it the first Constitution of Ukraine.

Illia Mechnykov - Immune System

Illia Mechnikov is the author of fundamental works in immunology, bacteriology, epidemiology, and studying the human ageing process. Mechnikov was born in 1845 in Ivanivka village, Kharkiv region, and later studied at the Kharkiv University. He also worked at the Odesa University and the Pasteur Institute in Paris. Illia Mechnikov was the first to develop a coherent theory of protective systems of the human body. For the development of the human immune system concept, he received the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.

Petro Prokopovych - Beekeeping

Before the Ukrainian inventor Petro Prokopovych invented the framework hive, the fixed-comb hives for beekeeping required harvesters to kill the whole bee colony to collect honey. Then, in 1814, Petro Prokopovych proposed the world’s first design of a movable frame beehive. It not only provided easy access to the honeycombs but also allowed honey collection without harming bees. Prokopovych’s beehive quickly spread around the world. Further modifications in Europe and the USA was based on the design and methods of Petro Prokopovych.

Shchedryk (Carol of the Bells)

Shchedryk is commonly known as "Carol of the Bells" in English. Shchedryk is a Ukrainian folk song with origins going back to pre-Christian times (Pagan era) of ancient Ukrainian culture. Ukrainians celebrated New Years in Spring when the swallows have returned home after winter and when greenery started to return to Ukraine. In 1916, Mykola Leontovych, a Ukrainian composer, arranged the Ukrainian folk song, and soon Shchedryk became known all over the world.

Ivan Puliui - X-ray

Ukrainian physicist, electrical engineer, inventor, translator, and community figure. Puliui made major contributions in the fields of electrical discharges in gases, molecular physics, cathode rays, and the technology of alternating electrical currents. While working with a cathode-ray tube of his own invention (widely known at the time as the ‘Puluj tube’), he observed and recorded on film ‘mysterious invisible rays’ several years prior to W. Roentgen's observation, but he published his results only after Roentgen published his. Pului was the first to develop the X-ray.

Ukrainian Dance around the World

Dance has been a vital part of the Ukrainian culture, which can be traced back to third millennium BC. In the middle of the 19th century Lviv region, which was called Halychyna at that time, was a well known in Europe oil-producing region. Oil was very cheap because there was plenty of it unlike alcohol, which was rather expensive and highly demanded substance. That is why the owner of «Under the Gold Star» Lviv pharmacy, located on Kopernyka street decided to distill alcohol out of oil. In 1853 he delegated this task to his two employees, Jan Zech and Ignacy Lukasiewicz.

Valentyn Sylvestrov

Ukrainian composer. In many of his works he adopted dodecaphonist and avant-garde techniques, at the same time retaining a characteristically lyrical expression. He used a ramified tonal system, including polytonal counterpoint, also introducing 12-tone serialism. Starting in the 1970s he employed polystylistic methods in which tonality, atonality, modality, texture, and audiovisual elements all enter into dialogue with each other, forming broadly based dramatic compositions.
